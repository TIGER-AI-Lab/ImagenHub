Abstract

We introduce ImagenHub, a one-stop library to standardize the inference and evaluation of all the conditional image generation models. Firstly, we define seven prominent tasks and curate high-quality evaluation datasets for them. Secondly, we built a unified inference pipeline to ensure fair comparison. Thirdly, we design two human evaluation scores, i.e. Seman- tic Consistency and Perceptual Quality, along with comprehensive guidelines to evaluate generated images. We train expert raters to evaluate the model outputs based on the proposed metrics. Our human evaluation achieves a high inter-worker agreement of Krippendorff‚Äôs alpha on 76% models with a value higher than 0.4. We comprehensively evaluated a total of around 30 models and observed three key takeaways: (1) the existing models‚Äô performance is generally unsatisfying except for Text-guided Image Generation and Subject-driven Image Generation, with 74% models achieving an overall score lower than 0.5. (2) we examined the claims from published papers and found 83% of them hold with a few exceptions. (3) None of the existing automatic metrics has a Spearman‚Äôs correlation higher than 0.2 except subject-driven image generation. Moving forward, we will con- tinue our efforts to evaluate newly published models and update our leaderboard to keep track of the progress in conditional image generation.

In order to better understand what factors contribute to the great gain of ImagenHub over existing baselines, we set up a group of control experiments in the Figure 3. We study the following setups:

  1. ImagenHub (MathInstruct - CoT): This experiment aims to understand how much our curated CoT data could improve the generalization over the SoTA model WizardMath trained specifically on GSM + MATH. As can be seen, while sacrificing accuracy on GSM + MATH by 3%, our CoT subset fine-tuning improves the overall nine-dataset accuracy from 27% to 32%.
  2. ImagenHub (MathInstruct - PoT): This experiment aims to understand the advantage of our PoT subset. As can be observed, our PoT subset fine-tuning can significantly improve the overall accuracy from 27% to 37.5%. This ablation reflects the importance of unlocking the program generation capabilities of our model.
  3. ImagenHub (MathInstruct - Hybrid): We further combine CoT and PoT as the hybrid training data to achieve the best overall performance of 45.4%. This combined gain comes from two aspects:
    • The CoT subset can help maintain the generic language-based reasoning skills to handle scenarios where PoT cannot handle well, e.g., the multi-choice questions in AQuA, SAT, and MMLU.
    • The PoT subset can teach the model how to utilize Python APIs to solve complex math problems with high precision, e.g., the MATH problems requiring complex computation.
